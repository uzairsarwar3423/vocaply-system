## 1. Pricing Philosophy & Strategy

### Core Pricing Decision: Flat Team Pricing (Not Per-Seat)

Vocaply uses **flat team pricing** — one price for a whole team, not per user.

```
THE DECISION:
  REJECTED:  Per-user/per-seat pricing ($X per person per month)
  CHOSEN:    Flat team pricing ($X per team per month, member limit)

WHY PER-SEAT WAS REJECTED:
  ✗ "Seat anxiety" — managers hesitate to add new teammates
  ✗ Every new hire = budget conversation = friction
  ✗ Teams actively avoid inviting people to avoid extra cost
  ✗ Kills the viral loop (you WANT teams to invite everyone)
  ✗ Vocaply's cost is driven by MEETINGS, not by USERS
  ✗ A 5-person team and a 10-person team with same meetings
    cost Vocaply nearly the same — per-seat doesn't reflect reality

WHY FLAT TEAM PRICING WINS:
  ✓ No seat anxiety — add your whole team freely
  ✓ Natural expansion: team grows → upgrade plan → more revenue
  ✓ Company expense = manager swipes card = no approval needed
  ✓ Predictable monthly cost = easy to budget
  ✓ Team adoption is incentivized (cost doesn't increase)
  ✓ Industry proven: Basecamp ($99/team), Linear (per-seat but
    very low), Notion (per-seat but team plans)
```

### Pricing Model: Value-Based + Usage-Gated

```
PRICING DRIVER:
  Primary gate:    Meetings per month (drives AI cost)
  Secondary gate:  Team members (drives support + storage cost)
  Value metric:    Hours saved per manager per month

THE GOLDEN RATIO:
  Value delivered to manager: 2.5 hours/week saved
  Manager's hourly cost:      $50–$150/hour (blended)
  Monthly value delivered:    2.5h × 4 weeks × $100 = $1,000/month
  Price charged:              $49–$199/month
  Value/Price ratio:          5x–20x ROI

CONCLUSION: Vocaply is dramatically underpriced relative to value.
  This is intentional at launch — win customers fast, prove value,
  raise prices gradually as brand and retention data compounds.
```

### Why Managers Pay (Not Individuals)

```
BUYER PROFILE:
  Who: Engineering Manager / Team Lead / Product Manager
  Budget type: SaaS tools budget (company expense)
  Decision threshold: < $100/month → no approval needed
  Decision threshold: < $200/month → light approval
  Decision threshold: > $200/month → procurement process

PSYCHOLOGICAL JUSTIFICATION:
  "Jira costs $8/user × 6 people = $48/month"
  "Vocaply costs $49 flat for all 6 — AND creates Jira tickets"
  → Vocaply pays for itself

  "Slack costs $7.25 × 10 people = $72.50/month"
  "Vocaply Growth costs $99 flat for 25 people"
  → Comparable to tools they already pay for

  "I spend 2.5 hours/week on follow-up emails"
  "At $80/hr that's $800/month of my time"
  "Vocaply costs $99/month"
  → 8x ROI on time alone
```

### Annual Strategy: Why 20% Discount is the Right Number

```
TOO LOW (< 10%):
  → Not compelling enough to change billing cycle
  → Customer math: "I save $12/year? Not worth it"
  → Annual adoption stays below 20%

TOO HIGH (> 30%):
  → Revenue cannibalization: monthly customers switch
  → Perceived as desperation: "Why are they offering 30% off?"
  → Cuts into margin significantly

20% IS THE SWEET SPOT:
  → Industry standard (Slack, Linear, Notion all use ~20%)
  → Compelling enough to motivate: "I save $240/year"
  → "2 months free" framing works psychologically
  → Annual churn drops from 8–12% to 1–2%

HOW TO FRAME:
  ❌ "Save 20%" (percentage is abstract)
  ✓  "Save $240/year" (dollar amount is concrete)
  ✓  "Get 2 months free" (feels like a gift)
  ✓  "Pay $79/month, billed annually" (focus on lower number)
```

### Annual Revenue Impact Model

```
SCENARIO: 30% of Growth plan customers switch to annual

Monthly scenario (all monthly):
  20 Growth teams × $99 = $1,980 MRR = $23,760 ARR

Mixed scenario (70% monthly, 30% annual):
  14 monthly  × $99  = $1,386 MRR
   6 annual   × $79  = $474 MRR (but $5,688 cash in January)
  Combined MRR = $1,860 (-$120/month)
  Cash collected upfront = $5,688

THE TRADEOFF:
  → You lose $120/month in recognized revenue
  → You gain $5,688 in January (cash flow positive)
  → Annual customers stay 6x longer (see churn model)
  → Net 12-month value of annual customer = $948 vs monthly customer at
    8% churn = $99 × (1/0.08) = $1,237 expected LTV

RECOMMENDATION:
  Offer annual prominently. Target 40% annual mix by Month 12.
  Use annual pricing to fund growth at early stage.
```

## 6. Variable Cost Per Meeting

### Cost Per Meeting Calculation

```
COST COMPONENTS PER MEETING:

Component 1 — Recall.ai Bot (LARGEST variable cost)
  Cost:       ~$0.70 per meeting hour
  Avg meeting: 30 minutes
  Cost/meeting: $0.70 × 0.5h = $0.35
  For 45 min:   $0.70 × 0.75h = $0.53
  For 60 min:   $0.70 × 1.0h  = $0.70

Component 2 — Claude API (AI Extraction)
  Model:      claude-haiku-4-5-20251001 (primary)
  Pricing:    $0.25/M input tokens, $1.25/M output tokens
  Avg transcript: ~6,000 tokens input, ~1,000 tokens output
  Cost per meeting:
    Input:   6,000 × $0.00000025 = $0.0015
    Output:  1,000 × $0.00000125 = $0.00125
    Total:   ~$0.003 per meeting (VERY cheap)

  Model:      claude-haiku-4-5-20251001 for summary
  Cost:       ~$0.005 per meeting

  Resolution check (cross-meeting, optional):
    Only runs if similarity > 0.65 threshold
    ~50% of meetings trigger this
    Cost: ~$0.001 × 0.5 = $0.0005

  Total Claude cost per meeting: ~$0.01–$0.03

Component 3 — Infrastructure (amortized per meeting)
  At 1,000 meetings/month, $400 fixed infra:
  Cost per meeting: $0.40

  At 10,000 meetings/month, $2,455 fixed infra:
  Cost per meeting: $0.25

  At 50,000 meetings/month, same infra (scales slowly):
  Cost per meeting: $0.05

TOTAL COST PER MEETING (30-min avg standup):
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Recall.ai bot:           $0.35
  Claude API:              $0.03
  Infrastructure (Phase 1):$0.40
  Infrastructure (Phase 2):$0.25
  Infrastructure (Phase 3):$0.05
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Phase 1 total:           ~$0.78 per meeting
  Phase 2 total:           ~$0.63 per meeting
  Phase 3 total:           ~$0.43 per meeting

  NOTE: As you scale, Recall.ai cost stays flat per meeting
  but infrastructure cost per meeting drops dramatically.
  Target at scale: < $0.50 total cost per meeting.

  Recall.ai volume discount (>10K hours/month):
    Standard:   $0.70/hour
    10K+ hours: ~$0.55/hour (est. negotiated)
    50K+ hours: ~$0.40/hour (est. negotiated)
```

### Net Revenue Retention (NRR) — How Existing Customers Grow Revenue

```
NET REVENUE RETENTION = (Starting MRR + Expansions - Contractions - Churn) / Starting MRR

EXPANSION SOURCES:
  1. Plan upgrades (Starter → Growth → Business)
  2. Member limit upgrades (buying next tier)
  3. Meeting overage purchases
  4. Annual plan conversions

EXAMPLE — Month 13 Starting Cohort of 30 Starter Teams:
  Starting MRR from cohort:    30 × $49  = $1,470

  By Month 25 (12 months later):
    8 churned (27% annual churn)                 = -$392 MRR
    5 upgraded to Growth ($99)                    = +$250 MRR expansion
    2 upgraded to Business ($199)                 = +$300 MRR expansion
   15 stayed on Starter ($49)                     = $735 MRR (stable)

  Ending MRR from cohort:  $735 + $250×$99 + $200×2 + $200
  Wait — let's recalculate:
    15 Starter:   15 × $49  = $735
    5 Growth:      5 × $99  = $495
    2 Business:    2 × $199 = $398
    8 churned:     8 × $0   = $0
    ─────────────────────────────
    Total ending MRR:        $1,628

  NRR = $1,628 / $1,470 = 110.7% ← EXCELLENT

  Month 12:  ~108%
```

## 16. Pricing Psychology & Conversion Strategy

### The $99 Sweet Spot — Why It Works

```
PRICE ANCHORING PSYCHOLOGY:

Step 1 — Show Business ($199) first:
  Visitor sees $199 → anchors to "expensive"
  Then sees Growth ($99) → feels like a deal
  
Step 2 — "Most Popular" badge on Growth:
  Social proof: everyone picks this one
  Reduces analysis paralysis
  
Step 3 — "Under $100" psychological barrier:
  $99 is under manager's no-approval threshold
  $100 would require a conversation with finance
  $99 never does — it's exactly $1 under the barrier
  
Step 4 — Per-member math:
  $99 / 25 members = $3.96/person
  "Less than a coffee per person per month"
  Reframes from "team expense" to "individual value"

THE DECOY EFFECT:
  Business ($199) exists partly as a decoy
  It makes Growth ($99) look reasonable by comparison
  Without Business, $99 would feel expensive
  With Business at $199, $99 feels like the smart choice
```

### Annual Plan Conversion Copy

```
WRONG FRAMING:           RIGHT FRAMING:
"Save 20%"               "Get 2 months free"
"Billed annually"        "Pay $79/month, billed as $948 once a year"
"Annual plan"            "Lock in your rate — prices may increase"
"20% off"                "You save $240 this year"

ANNUAL PLAN TIMING:
  Best time to offer annual:
  → After user's first "aha moment" (first meeting processed)
  → At 30-day mark (before first monthly renewal decision)
  → When team shows expansion intent (inviting 3rd member)
  → After Jira integration is connected (high switching cost)
```

### Free Trial Strategy

```
TRIAL DESIGN:
  Duration:     14 days (not 7, not 30)
  Credit card:  NOT required (removes primary barrier)
  Plan:         All features unlocked (they experience full value)
  Limit:        10 meetings during trial (enough to see 2+ standups)
  
WHY 14 DAYS:
  7 days: Too short — team may not have enough meetings
  14 days: Two full work weeks — they see value in both sprints
  30 days: Too long — creates procrastination, lower urgency

TRIAL → PAID CONVERSION TARGET:
  Industry average B2B SaaS trial conversion: 15–25%
```

## 20. Risk Analysis & Sensitivity Model

### Revenue Risk Scenarios

```
SCENARIO              PROBABILITY  M12 MRR    M24 MRR   MITIGATION
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Base case             50%          $4,445     $25,127   Execute plan as written
Upside case (+30%)    20%          $5,779     $32,665   Product Hunt success,
                                                         viral growth, PR hit
Downside case (-30%)  25%          $3,112     $17,589   Slower adoption, more
                                                         competition, AI cost rise
Severe downside (-60%)5%           $1,778     $10,051   Major competitor copies,
                                                         Recall.ai price surge

MAJOR FINANCIAL RISKS:

Risk 1 — Recall.ai price increase:
  Current:    $0.70/hour
  Scenario:   Recall raises to $1.50/hour
  Impact:     Variable cost doubles → margins drop 15–20%
  Mitigation: Negotiate volume discounts early. Build backup bot.
              Add overage charges to offset. Raise prices 10–15%.

Risk 2 — Claude API cost increase:
  Current:    ~$0.03/meeting
  Scenario:   Anthropic raises prices 3x
  Impact:     $0.09/meeting additional cost
  Mitigation: At current prices, minimal impact. Even 10x increase
              only adds $0.27/meeting. Claude cost is not a
              significant margin risk at current pricing.

Risk 3 — High churn (above 8%/month):
  Scenario:   Poor retention → churn >8% monthly
  Impact:     Growth stalls, can't reach $5K MRR
  Mitigation: Double down on weekly digest + Slack integration.
              Monthly churn above 6% triggers product team review.
              Offer pause option (vs cancel) for budget-constrained teams.

Risk 4 — Competitor launches at same price:
  Scenario:   Fireflies copies commitment tracking
  Impact:     Slower new customer growth, pricing pressure
  Mitigation: Cross-meeting memory (resolver algorithm) is 3–6
              months to build properly. First-mover advantage real.
              Focus on brand + trust moat, not just features.

Risk 5 — No trial conversion (<15%):
  Scenario:   Trial signups don't convert
  Impact:     High marketing cost, low return
  Mitigation: Indicates product or onboarding problem, not pricing.
              Fix: reduce time to first "aha moment" (auto-join first
              meeting on calendar day 1 of trial).
```
